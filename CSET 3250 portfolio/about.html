About Me

My name is Cody Foley, and I am an Information Technology student at the University of Toledo with a focus on networking and cybersecurity. I am currently working toward gaining real-world experience in IT support and security while continuing to develop my technical and problem-solving skills.

I previously served in the United States Army as a Fire Support Specialist, where I gained extensive experience in communication systems, data analysis, and leadership. During my service, I operated and maintained critical communication equipment, worked with advanced systems such as AFATADS, and coordinated operations while supervising teams of soldiers.

My technical experience includes working with HTML, Java, Python, and database systems, along with developing web-based applications and completing cybersecurity labs involving system analysis and vulnerability assessment. I am a quick learner who enjoys solving complex problems and adapting to new technologies.

This portfolio showcases my academic work, technical projects, and skills, including projects such as NetworkBuddy and database-driven web applications. My goal is to continue growing in the IT field and contribute to cybersecurity and network infrastructure environments.

Key Strengths

  • Networking and cybersecurity fundamentals
  • Experience with databases and web development
  • Strong communication and leadership background
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking skills
